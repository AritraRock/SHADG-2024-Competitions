Operheimer - Operations Modelling Challenge: Stages and Timelines

IIT Kharagpur's prestigious Techno-Optimisation Fest, Optima 2023 in association with ORMAE, proudly presents Operations Modelling Challenge, an exciting competition designed to test participants' business thinking, operations management skills, and their ability to develop sophisticated operations models using optimisation software. This competition comprises two challenging rounds, allowing participants to showcase their expertise and compete for top honours.

ORMAE Group is a rich talent pool of scientists, that delivers cost-effective Optimisation and Data Science solutions in diverse business domains with direct impact on Revenues. 

This has been made possible with the sincere efforts of theDepartment of Industrial and Systems Engineering, IIT Kharagpur.

Round 1-

Case Description: Participants will receive a real-world business scenario or problem related to operations management. The case will present a set of challenges and opportunities that require innovative solutions. Participants will be given access to relevant data and information.

Task: Participants are required to analyse the case, identify key issues, propose strategic solutions, and outline a comprehensive operations management plan. Submissions should include a detailed written report, along with supporting data, charts, and visuals.

Evaluation: Submissions will be evaluated based on the quality of analysis, feasibility of proposed solutions, innovation, and clarity of communication. A panel of expert judges will assess the submissions and shortlist the top teams to advance to Round 2.

Round 2-

Case Presentation: Shortlisted teams will be invited to present their Round 1 solutions and strategies to a panel of judges in a live or virtual setting. This presentation should highlight the practicality and potential impact of their proposed solutions.

Operations Model Development: Participants will be provided with access to optimisation software such as CPLEX, Gurobi, or other relevant tools. They will be given a new set of data and parameters related to the same case. Teams must develop operations models that optimise key aspects of the business operations based on the given constraints.

Task: Teams will need to create and implement operations models that maximise efficiency, reduce costs, or achieve specific business objectives. The models should be robust, scalable, and adaptable to changing conditions.

Evaluation: Judges will assess the quality of the case presentation, the effectiveness of the operations models, and the overall feasibility and impact of the proposed solutions. Teams will be scored based on their ability to optimise the given problem using the chosen software.
